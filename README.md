# Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze customer shopping behavior and purchasing patterns. The dashboard helps businesses understand customer demographics, spending habits, product preferences, subscription trends, and sales performance to support data-driven decision-making.

## Business Objective

The objective of this project is to:

* Analyze customer purchasing behavior
* Identify high-value customer segments
* Evaluate product category performance
* Understand subscription and payment preferences
* Generate actionable business insights

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains customer-level shopping data, including:

* Customer ID
* Age
* Gender
* Category
* Purchase Amount (USD)
* Review Rating
* Subscription Status
* Payment Method
* Shipping Type
* Season
* Discount Applied
* Frequency of Purchases

Total Records: **3,900 Customers**

## Data Preparation

### Power Query

* Checked data quality
* Verified data types
* Prepared data for analysis

### Calculated Column

Created an **Age Group** column to segment customers into:

* Young
* Adult
* Middle Age
* Senior

### DAX Measures

Created measures for:

* Total Customers
* Total Purchase Amount

## Key Insights

* Clothing is the highest-performing category in terms of customer count and revenue.
* Middle Age customers contribute the largest share of sales.
* Most customers are non-subscribers, indicating an opportunity for loyalty programs.
* Credit Card and PayPal are the most preferred payment methods.
* Average customer purchase amount is approximately **$59.76**.
* Average review rating is **3.75**, indicating moderate customer satisfaction.

## Business Recommendations

1. Increase subscription adoption through loyalty rewards.
2. Focus marketing efforts on high-value customer segments.
3. Expand and promote top-performing product categories.
4. Improve customer experience to increase review ratings.
5. Implement seasonal campaigns to boost sales during low-performing periods.
